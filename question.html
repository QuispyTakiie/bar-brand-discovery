BRAND DISCOVERY BAR PROJECT · DAY ZERO

Before the bar,
the brand.

Six short sections. Most are deliberately short — the hard part is committing to one answer, not writing long ones. Take 60 to 90 minutes, in one sitting.

Before we get into business model, costs, location, or anything operational, we need to lock the brand. Everything else — pricing, customer, menu direction, marketing, even where the bar should physically be — gets easier once the brand is clear. Without it, every decision later becomes a 50/50 coin flip.

This is a forcing exercise. If you finish this with strong answers, the next phase (BMC, SWOT, customer segmentation) moves fast. If you can't answer something — that's the most useful signal of all. Mark it and we'll talk.

Ground Rules

  • Answer in Thai or English — whichever you think in. Mix is fine.
  • Don't give multiple options for a single question. Pick one. If you can't decide, just write "can't decide" — that's a signal we work with.
  • Don't over-research. First-instinct answers reveal more than polished ones.
  • Your progress auto-saves to this browser. You can close and come back.

Brand Essence Statement

WHY THIS MATTERS If you can't fill in this one sentence, the brand isn't real yet. This is the single most important answer in the workbook.

"We are the bar in that for , so they feel ."

Guidance per blank

[archetype]
Use your answer from Section 1 (e.g., "Explorer" or "Lover-Creator").
[neighborhood]
Be specific. Not "Bangkok." Name the soi or sub-area.
[unique action]
The thing only this bar does. Not "serves drinks." Specific — "builds cocktails around overlooked Thai herbs," "plays one full vinyl album per night," "has no menu — bartender reads the room."
[tribe]
A narrow group. Not "everyone." Try age band + occupation/lifestyle + nationality mix.
[emotional payoff]
How guests feel walking out. Not "happy." Specific — "like an insider," "unhurried for the first time all week," "smarter than when they walked in."

The Anti-Brand

WHY THIS MATTERS This kills the scattered ideas. Every "what if we also did…" thought that doesn't survive this section gets cut. It's painful but it's the fastest way to focus.

Three things this bar will absolutely NOT be.

Examples: "Not a sports bar." "Not a place for groups of 8+." "Not cheap — we will not compete on price." "Not loud after 11pm." "Not Instagram-bait."

05

Brand Promise

WHY THIS MATTERS This is the single experience guarantee every guest leaves with. It's what you stake your reputation on. Operations, hiring, training, and quality control all answer to this one line.

5.1
The one thing every guest should be able to say after their visit.

Bad answers (too generic): "Great drinks." "Good vibes."
Good answers (specific, defensible): "Best Negroni variation in Bangna." "The bartender remembered my name on my second visit." "I learned something new about a spirit I thought I knew."

5.2
How will you actually deliver this every single night? What has to be true operationally?
06

Tone of Voice

WHY THIS MATTERS This dictates how the brand speaks — on the menu, on Instagram, on the website, even how the staff greet guests. Three sharp adjectives lock this down.

BANNED WORDS — "fun," "welcoming," "premium," "unique," "cozy," "vibrant." These are filler. Force yourself to pick adjectives that actually distinguish.

Good examples (texture, not filler): "wry, generous, unhurried" / "sharp, irreverent, knowledgeable" / "warm, precise, low-key."
